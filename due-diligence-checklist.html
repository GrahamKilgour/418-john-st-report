A

Go / No-Go Gating Checks (Highest Priority)

Complete these items before spending on design. Any one of these can change unit yield, block access, or kill the deal entirely.

A1. Environmental / Active Auto Shop Risk (Possible Deal-Killer)

Why it matters: Because you have an active automotive shop, the environmental path is likely the single biggest uncertainty for cost + timeline + financing. The Secondary Plan explicitly ties development permissions/holding provisions to environmental remediation and submission of a Record of Site Condition in some contexts. Even where a formal "H" isn’t on your parcel, the City commonly conditions approvals on ESA outcomes when there’s a known/likely contaminating use history.

What to Obtain (Download / Order)

  • Phase I ESA (new, addressed to you/financier if possible) CSA standard. Desktop review of historical uses, site reconnaissance, interviews.
  • Phase II ESA if recommended by Phase I Soil/groundwater sampling. Likely given active automotive use.
  • RSC pathway confirmation If redevelopment involves a change to more sensitive use (residential intensification), confirm whether a Record of Site Condition is required and what standards apply. Get the City’s position in writing.
Immediate Question

Is the shop strictly storage/repair, or bodywork/painting, or fluid handling? This changes expected contaminants and remediation scope.

Decision Gates
  • Remediation costs/timeline exceed threshold.
  • Vapour intrusion / soil removal constraints materially change build design or cost.

A2. Legal Non-Conforming / Non-Complying Status of the Auto Shop

Why it matters: The current D District is "Urban Protected Residential – One and Two Family Dwellings, Etc." The auto shop likely exists as legal non-conforming (or as some form of accessory/previous approval). This affects: whether you can keep operating the shop until redevelopment, whether the shop can be expanded/altered, and whether the City will treat redevelopment as a straightforward conversion or a more complex change-of-use file.

What to Obtain (Download / Request)

  • City building permit history For both the house and the shop (construction date, use, alterations).
  • Business licensing record / property standards orders If any exist.
  • Prior Committee of Adjustment decisions, variances, or permissions on title Any City letters, old permits, or prior approvals.
Policy Hook (Secondary Plan)

The Secondary Plan has policies about new development adjacent to non-complying uses needing mitigation (noise, dust, vibration, odour, etc.).

A3. Confirm Lot Fabric + Legal Constraints (Survey + Title)

Why it matters: Even with confirmed frontage/depth, the legal evidence is needed because small differences change whether "two-family" standards are met cleanly and whether severance is realistic.

What to Obtain (Download / Order)

  • Up-to-date boundary survey (OLS) Including existing buildings/footprints, grades, driveway location/width, fences/retaining walls, trees (diameter/condition), visible utility features, any encroachments.
  • Parcel Register / PIN (Teranet) and any reference plan Confirm legal description, dimensions, instruments, easements/ROWs, restrictive covenants.
  • Easements / rights-of-way Especially over/under the driveway, encroachments, and any shared servicing.
What This Directly Affects
  • Whether you truly meet the two-family minimum lot width cleanly (planner memo says yes).
  • Whether a severance is physically/legally possible (not assumed).
  • Whether you can preserve a compliant access route to rear parking / fire access.

A4. Confirm the Actual Controlling Policy Lens

Why it matters: The Secondary Plan Low Density Residential policies specify permitted built forms (single detached, semi-detached, street townhouses), a density range (25–60 uph), and direction on compatibility/context (reflecting existing low density character, consistent lot dimensions and setbacks, garages at rear accessed from rear laneways "where feasible").

Due Diligence Task

  • Pull Schedule M-2 mapping page for your exact parcel Confirm Low Density Residential designation (planner memo says yes).
  • Check for site-specific policy areas Some sites have unique permissions like multiple dwellings / higher densities. Confirm whether any apply.

Zoning Due Diligence — Current "D District" (By-law 6593)

B1. Confirm D District Standards + "Max Units on a Lot" Cap

The planner memo’s "as-of-right" conclusion is:

Key Finding — 4-Unit Cap

Highest and best use as-of-right: two-family (duplex or semi) + secondary units → 4 units possible.

By-law 6593 Section 19 includes a clear statement that no more than four dwelling units are permitted on a lot in a "D" District (among others).

Due Diligence Task

  • Confirm whether any site-specific exceptions apply to your parcel Your GIS pop-up shows "Exception 1" fields but doesn’t display details — this must be verified against the actual exception schedule/by-law.

B2. Secondary Dwelling Units (Internal + Detached) — Confirm Design Constraints

Why it matters: By-law 6593 Section 19 defines and regulates both types of secondary dwelling units. These rules determine whether you can actually reach 4 units on this lot and what site-planning constraints apply.

Key SDU regulations from By-law 6593 Section 19:

Rule Requirement
SDU (internal) Within the principal dwelling
SDU – Detached Detached unit on same lot
Parking (general) No additional parking required
Parking (4th unit, detached) 1 parking space required if the detached SDU is the 4th dwelling unit
Location (detached SDU) Only in rear and/or interior side yard
Setbacks (detached SDU) Min 1.2 m from interior side and rear lot lines
Access path Unobstructed path from street line to entrance (min width/height)
Max height (detached SDU) 6.0 m
Max GFA Subject to by-law GFA rules (confirm exact metric in full clause)

Due Diligence Task — Concept Sketch Required

Before "max unit count" is underwritten, produce a quick concept sketch showing:

  • Where a detached SDU could fit without killing driveway access Show the 1.2 m setbacks, building separation, and remaining access width.
  • How the unobstructed path from street to entrance works Must meet minimum width/height requirements per the by-law.
  • Whether parking is triggered If the detached SDU is unit #4, 1 parking space is required. Show where it fits.
C

Parking & Access Due Diligence

Critical for "maximum unit count" even when parking is "not required." Site logistics can cap what’s buildable.

C1. Confirm Parking Rate Area Status (PRA 1 Claim)

Why it matters: The planner memo asserts the parcel is in Parking Rate Area 1 under By-law 05-200, with "no parking required for most residential uses."

Parking By-law (05-200 Section 5) shows that in PRA 1:

Use Type PRA 1 Requirement
Triplex / Fourplex / Duplex / Dwelling Unit (under 5 units) No parking spaces required
Multiple dwelling (5+ units) — resident parking No resident parking required
Multiple dwelling (5+ units) — visitor parking 2 spaces + additional per-unit rate

Due Diligence Tasks

  • Pull the City’s official Parking Rate Area map printout for the parcel Save as PDF. This is the single most impactful confirmation for site layout feasibility.
  • Even where parking is not required by zoning, confirm: Market expectations, garbage staging, fire access turning/clearance, and whether front yard parking will be opposed (planner memo warns front-yard parking for multi-unit is likely not supported).
Decision Gate

If not PRA1, parking requirements may materially reduce achievable unit count or require variances.

C2. Driveway Geometry (Private Driveway = Major Asset — Verify It)

Why it matters: Your private driveway is a significant asset for site access and rear parking. But it must be confirmed as fully on-title and geometrically workable.

From By-law 6593:

Standard Requirement
Parking stall minimum (non-parallel) 2.7 m × 6.0 m
90° maneuvering aisle 6.0 m
Driveway width Varies by number of spaces; backing limitations may apply

What to Confirm with Survey + Concept Sketch

  • Clear width, setbacks, and whether driveway is fully on-title Even "private" driveways sometimes have easements.
  • Whether it can support: Rear parking arrangement, construction access, fire department access expectations (even if not a formal fire route).

C3. Fire Access and Building Code Feasibility

Why it matters: Even if zoning and parking are fine, exiting, fire separations, and access can cap the number of units. Part 9 vs Part 3 threshold is critical for cost.

Get

  • Quick code sanity check by a designer Part 9 / Part 3 threshold issues depending on size, height, and unit count.
  • Fire/Building Division pre-consultation (or code consultant) Access routes, hydrant proximity, fire department connection placement.

C4. Waste Collection Feasibility

Get

  • City waste pickup requirements for small multi-unit buildings Bin size, set-out protocol, collection point requirements. R1a fourplex rules address waste storage location (enclosed, not in front yard).
  • Concept plan showing compliant waste enclosure Integrated into the site layout concept.

Approvals & Process Due Diligence

What path matches what unit count?

E1. As-of-Right Baseline (Confirm, Then Underwrite)

Current Position

Under current D District + Secondary Plan LDR, the planner memo’s as-of-right position is 4 units (two-family + secondary units). By-law 6593 supports the 4-unit cap in D District lots where secondary units apply.

Due Diligence — Produce a Concept That Meets:

  • Secondary Plan compatibility policies Scale/character, setbacks context, rear garage access "where feasible."
  • Secondary-unit standards (if used for units 3 + 4) Internal SDU and/or detached SDU per By-law 6593 Section 19 requirements.

E2. Minor Variance / Consent / Amendments (If Targeting 6–8+ Units)

Context: Planner memo suggests 6–8 units may require OPA/ZBA and strong design/parking solutions. Street townhouses are noted as excluded in D District within this Secondary Plan area (per the planner’s reading/interpretation).

Planning Act s.45 allows "minor variance" from the zoning by-law. The four tests:

  • Desirable for appropriate development
  • Maintains general intent and purpose of the by-law
  • Maintains general intent and purpose of the official plan
  • Is minor in nature

Due Diligence Tasks (If Chasing >4 Units Under D)

  • Pre-consult with City Planning Get written notes. Understand staff position on intensity in this Stable Area context.
  • Build a "four tests" rationale package Only after you have a concept plan, streetscape context, and the environmental path is understood.
  • 10–20 nearby CoA decisions Minor variances + consents within the same Secondary Plan area, preferably within a few blocks. Look for: reduced lot widths, reduced side yards, multi-unit permissions, parking variances. This is your strongest predictor of success.

Download List & Overlays

Must-Have (Developer-Ready Report Requires These)

  1. Survey (OLS) + legal description
  2. Parcel Register / PIN + reference plan(s)
  3. City GIS printouts (as PDFs/screenshots) for:
    • Zoning & exceptions layer (showing any exception numbers)

  5. Phase I ESA (and Phase II if triggered)
  6. Servicing info (water, sanitary, storm; any combined sewer notes)
  7. Heritage register check (whether listed/designated; and any heritage district)

Strongly Recommended (Reduces Surprise Conditions)

  1. Conservation Authority regulated area map (HCA) + floodplain/hazard layers
  2. Tree inventory / arborist letter (if mature trees constrain buildable area)
  3. Transportation / road widening info for John St N (Secondary Plan notes setbacks must account for required widenings)

Other Critical Overlays

Heritage / Cultural Heritage Landscapes

  • Hamilton heritage register lookup For the address and the immediate block.
Decision Gate

Heritage designation/listing can add cost/time and constrain demolition and new-build form.

Conservation Authority Regulated Areas / Floodplain

  • HCA regulated area mapping screenshot For the parcel. Hamilton Conservation Authority mapping.
Decision Gate

Any regulated area can trigger additional permits and constrain building footprint.

Rail Noise/Vibration Proximity Triggers

  • Map showing rail distance Secondary Plan references feasibility studies related to noise/vibration within 500 m of rail corridors. Confirm whether studies are required for your application type.

Targeted Questions (To Avoid Wrong Assumptions)

Three Questions to Answer
  1. Rear laneway: Is there any rear lane access behind the lot, or is access only via the front driveway? (Secondary Plan prefers rear garage access "where feasible.")
  2. Driveway location: Is the driveway fully on your lot, or does it rely on any shared/right-of-way arrangement? (Even "private" driveways sometimes have easements.)
  3. Shop operations: Any painting/bodywork, or strictly mechanical? Any known spills or underground storage historically?
