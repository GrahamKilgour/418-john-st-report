Secondary Plan & Policy Context

West Harbour (Setting Sail) Secondary Plan — Intent Signal

Low Density Residential Designation

The Secondary Plan identifies "Low Density Residential" areas and provides a density range of 25–60 units per gross hectare.

Stable Areas Policy Direction

Stable Areas are intended to remain stable; "significant physical change…is not anticipated." Development may occur, but it must be "compatible with the existing land use pattern and built form character." The plan supports "sensitive and compatible infill development" and encourages intensification where it respects context and compatibility.

04

Scenario A — "D" District (By-law 6593)

Permitted Uses in D District (Strict Reading)

In Section TEN — "D" Districts (Urban Protected Residential — One and Two Family Dwellings, etc.), permitted uses include:

  • (i) Private single-family dwelling
  • (ii) Two-family dwelling
  • Additional listed uses: kindergarten, child care centre, boarding/lodging/rooming house, church, public library, public park, private club, etc.
Key Constraint

The by-law's named residential forms in D are single-family and two-family only. The "boarding/lodging/rooming house" permission increases intensity as "rooms," but these are not self-contained dwelling units and carry different building/fire/operations implications.

Bottom Line — By-Right Unit Count (D District)

By-right maximum self-contained dwelling units = 2 (a two-family dwelling), based on the explicit permitted residential forms.

If achieving 4 units relies on "secondary units/ADUs," that authority is not established in the D District text and must be validated in Phase 2 against whatever city-wide ADU permissions apply to Former Hamilton zones.

Critical Design Implication

You can build up to 14 m, but exceeding 11 m or 2.5 storeys triggers much larger side yards (2.7 m vs 1.2 m), which can significantly reduce usable floorplate width. This is the main reason "3 storeys" is not always practically achievable on narrower lots.

Maximum New-Build Unit Yield (Entitlement-Driven)

A) Max By-Right (Strict D District)

Two-family dwelling (2 units) is the clear maximum self-contained unit count by right.

B) Max Likely Defensible Without Re-designation

Because the Secondary Plan Low Density range translates to about 2–5 units on this lot, the policy-aligned maximum is likely:

  • 2 units by-right, and possibly 3–4 units only if there is a confirmed mechanism allowing additional self-contained units (e.g., lawful secondary units/ADUs applicable to this zone)
To Confirm

The actual city-wide ADU / additional-unit permissions that apply to Former Hamilton By-law 6593 lands.

C) Maximum the Planner Memo Believes Possible (Requires OPA + ZBA)

The planner memo conclusion states:

  • Site can accommodate a two-family dwelling as-of-right
  • Minor variances "may allow" street townhouse units
  • Landwise opinion: a higher-density form with 6–8 units would require an Official Plan Amendment + Zoning By-law Amendment
Important

The 6–8 unit position is not "by-right D zoning" — it is an approval-dependent target requiring applications, fees, and City staff/Council support.

Scenario B — "R1a" Zone (By-law 05-200)

Permitted Uses in R1a

In 15.2.1.1 (Permitted Uses) — Low Density Residential, Small Lot (R1a) Zone, permitted residential forms include:

  • Single Detached Dwelling
  • Semi-Detached Dwelling
  • Duplex Dwelling
  • Triplex Dwelling
  • Fourplex Dwelling

Plus accessory uses/buildings and home business, etc.

Fourplex Dwelling = 4 units by-right, assuming compliance with R1a regulations. Street townhouse could produce 3 units across the frontage, but fourplex is typically the "max units per lot" form in this zone without additional permissions.

Gating Risks

9.1 Environmental (Likely the #1 Schedule/Cost Risk)

Record of Site Condition Required

Because of the existing automotive use, a Record of Site Condition will be required to support redevelopment. The planner memo recommends starting environmental work early. This is likely the single biggest variable for both timeline and budget.

9.2 Approvals Pathway Risk (If Chasing 6–8 Units)

The planner memo conclusion is clear: 6–8 units would require Official Plan Amendment + Zoning By-law Amendment. This means additional time, cost, and uncertainty relative to the by-right scenarios.

9.3 Minor Variance Legal Test

Under the Planning Act, a committee may authorize a minor variance if it is desirable for appropriate development and maintains the general intent and purpose of the by-law and official plan. This test will be applied explicitly in Phase 2 when the approvals strategy is built.
